# Typography Strategy: The Stately Chronicle

Our typographic system is designed to provide a sense of permanence and understated authority. It moves away from jarring weights and decorative italics toward a "Substantial Balance" strategy: using deliberate, mid-to-high weight pairings that feel grounded and professional.

---

## 1. The Hero: Newsreader (Serif)
**Role:** Brand Identity, Headlines, and Ritualistic Moments.
**Why:** Newsreader is a high-contrast serif that evokes the feeling of a master's ledger. By restricting its weight and removing italics, it gains a stately, architectural presence.

*   **Display Large (Hero Headlines):** 
    *   *Weight:* 600 (Semi-Bold) or 700 (Bold)
    *   *Style:* Normal (Strictly No Italics)
    *   *Tracking:* -1% (Slightly Tight)
*   **Headline Medium (Section Headers):**
    *   *Weight:* 500 (Medium) or 600 (Semi-Bold)
    *   *Tracking:* 0%
*   **Quotes & Hints:**
    *   *Weight:* 500 (Medium)
    *   *Style:* Normal (Strictly No Italics)

---

## 2. The Workhorse: Manrope (Sans-Serif)
**Role:** Body Text, Form Labels, and Complex Data.
**Why:** Manrope provides clean, geometric legibility. We avoid "Thin" or "Light" weights to maintain the tactile "Workbench" feel, ensuring every functional element has physical presence.

*   **Body Text:**
    *   *Weight:* 500 (Medium)
    *   *Line Height:* 1.6
*   **UI Labels & Button Text:**
    *   *Weight:* 600 (Semi-Bold)
    *   *Casing:* Uppercase with +8% letter spacing for an airy, premium feel.
*   **Metadata (Dates, Small Stats):**
    *   *Weight:* 500 (Medium)
    *   *Size:* 11px - 12px

---

## 3. The Ledger: JetBrains Mono (Monospace)
**Role:** Timers, Counts, and "Hard" Data.
**Why:** Monospaced fonts ensure that numbers don't "jump" as they change. It maintains a technical, sharp edge.

*   **Timer Display:**
    *   *Weight:* 600 (Semi-Bold)
    *   *Tracking:* 0%

---

## 4. Hierarchy & Refinement Strategy
1.  **Grounded Narrative:** Use Medium/Semi-Bold Serifs for the *story* of the work. Avoid weights above 700 to prevent a "noisy" or over-themed aesthetic.
2.  **Substantial Utility:** All functional text starts at 500 (Medium). No thin fonts are allowed, ensuring the UI feels "built" and reliable.
3.  **Strict Verticality:** No italics. Stateliness is achieved through clean, vertical letterforms.
4.  **Sophisticated Contrast:** Pair a Semi-Bold Serif headline with a Medium Sans-Serif body for a professional, high-contrast hierarchy that is easy on the eyes.
